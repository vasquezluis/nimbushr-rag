# NimbusHR Employee Onboarding Guide

**Version 2.3 | Last Updated: January 2025**

## Welcome to NimbusHR!

This guide outlines the complete onboarding process for new employees at NimbusHR. Our goal is to ensure every team member has a smooth transition and feels prepared to contribute from day one.

---

## Pre-Boarding (Before Day 1)

### Week Before Start Date

- **HR Paperwork**: Complete I-9, W-4, state tax forms, and benefits enrollment via the employee portal
- **Background Check**: Ensure all background check requirements are completed
- **Equipment Order**: IT will ship laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, and headset to your home address
- **Welcome Package**: You'll receive a NimbusHR swag box including company t-shirt, notebook, water bottle, and welcome letter from your manager

### 3 Days Before Start Date

- **Welcome Email**: Receive detailed first-day instructions including Zoom links and schedule
- **Manager Introduction**: Your manager will send a personal welcome message and schedule a 1:1 coffee chat
- **Buddy Assignment**: You'll be paired with an onboarding buddy who will be your go-to person for questions

---

## Week 1: Foundation & Orientation

### Day 1: Welcome & Setup

#### Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)

- **9:00 AM - Welcome Call**: Join the company-wide welcome call with HR and your cohort
- **10:00 AM - Account Setup**
  - Receive email invitation to access NimbusHR systems
  - Set up company email (Google Workspace)
  - Configure Slack profile and join relevant channels
  - Install required software: Zoom, 1Password, VPN client
- **11:00 AM - IT Orientation**: Technical setup walkthrough and troubleshooting

#### Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM)

- **1:00 PM - Company Overview**: CEO or senior leader presents company mission, values, and roadmap
- **2:00 PM - Benefits Overview**: HR walks through health insurance, 401(k), PTO policies, and perks
- **3:00 PM - Security Training Kickoff**: Begin mandatory security awareness training (Part 1 of 3)
- **4:00 PM - Meet Your Buddy**: Informal chat with your assigned onboarding buddy

### Day 2: Product & Culture

#### Morning

- **9:00 AM - Product Demo**: Deep dive into NimbusHR platform features and customer use cases
- **10:30 AM - Customer Success Stories**: Watch recorded customer testimonials and case studies
- **11:30 AM - Security Training (Part 2)**: Continue mandatory security awareness modules

#### Afternoon

- **1:00 PM - Department Overview**: Your department lead presents team structure, goals, and current projects
- **2:00 PM - Values & Culture**: Interactive session on NimbusHR's core values and workplace culture
- **3:00 PM - Team Introduction**: Employees are introduced to their team during the weekly onboarding call
- **4:00 PM - 1:1 with Manager**: Review 30-60-90 day plan and expectations

### Day 3: Tools & Access

#### Morning

- **9:00 AM - Communication Tools**: Training on Slack etiquette, email best practices, and meeting guidelines
- **10:00 AM - Documentation Hub**: Navigate Notion workspace, Google Drive structure, and knowledge base
- **11:00 AM - Security Training (Part 3)**: Complete mandatory security awareness training and certification

#### Afternoon

- **1:00 PM - Role-Specific Tool Access**:
  - **Engineering**: Access to GitHub, CI/CD pipelines (CircleCI), monitoring dashboards (Datadog, Sentry), AWS console (limited), Jira for project tracking
  - **Product**: Figma, Productboard, analytics tools (Mixpanel, Amplitude), user testing platforms
  - **Sales**: Salesforce CRM, Outreach.io, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Gong for call recording
  - **Customer Success**: Zendesk, Intercom, ChurnZero, customer health dashboards
  - **Marketing**: HubSpot, Google Analytics, SEMrush, Canva, content calendar tools
  - **Finance/Operations**: NetSuite, Expensify, Bill.com, reporting dashboards
- **3:00 PM - Tool Training**: Hands-on training for your role-specific tools
- **4:00 PM - Shadow Session**: Observe a team member doing their daily work

### Day 4: Deep Dive

#### Morning

- **9:00 AM - Customer Personas**: Learn about our ideal customer profile and buyer personas
- **10:00 AM - Competitive Landscape**: Overview of competitors and our differentiation strategy
- **11:00 AM - Compliance Training**: GDPR, SOC 2, data privacy, and industry regulations

#### Afternoon

- **1:00 PM - Cross-Functional Meetings**: Attend team standups or planning meetings as an observer
- **2:00 PM - First Assignment**: Receive your first small project or task to complete by end of week
- **3:30 PM - Q&A with Leadership**: Open forum with executive team members
- **4:30 PM - Check-in with Buddy**: Review week's progress and answer questions

### Day 5: Integration & Feedback

#### Morning

- **9:00 AM - Complete First Assignment**: Work time for your initial project
- **10:30 AM - Peer Shadowing**: Shadow 2-3 different team members in various roles
- **11:30 AM - Company All-Hands**: Join the weekly company-wide meeting (Fridays at 11:30 AM)

#### Afternoon

- **1:00 PM - Present First Assignment**: Quick presentation or demo of your completed work
- **2:00 PM - Feedback Session**: Structured feedback conversation with manager
- **3:00 PM - Week 1 Reflection**: Complete onboarding survey and self-assessment
- **4:00 PM - Social Time**: Optional virtual or in-person team social activity

## Week 2-4: Ramp-Up & Contribution

### Week 2 Goals

- **Ownership**: Take ownership of specific tasks or projects aligned with your role
- **Meetings**: Begin attending all relevant recurring meetings (standups, sprint planning, etc.)
- **Relationships**: Schedule 1:1 coffee chats with 5-7 cross-functional colleagues
- **Learning**: Complete role-specific training modules in the learning management system
- **Milestone**: Ship your first meaningful contribution (code, content, deal activity, etc.)

### Week 3 Goals

- **Independence**: Work independently on assigned projects with minimal guidance
- **Feedback**: Participate in your first code review, content review, or deal review
- **Process**: Understand and follow team workflows and approval processes
- **Documentation**: Contribute to or update internal documentation
- **Milestone**: Complete NimbusHR Product Certification (online exam)

### Week 4 Goals

- **Performance**: Operating at 50-60% of full productivity level
- **Initiative**: Identify and propose one improvement to existing processes or products
- **Collaboration**: Actively participate in team discussions and decision-making
- **30-Day Review**: Formal check-in with manager to review progress against 30-day goals
- **Milestone**: Complete your probationary period paperwork and review

---

## 30-60-90 Day Milestones

### 30 Days

- [ ] Complete all mandatory training and certifications
- [ ] Understand company products, customers, and competitive positioning
- [ ] Build relationships with immediate team and key stakeholders
- [ ] Deliver first meaningful contribution to team goals
- [ ] Participate in team meetings and provide input
- [ ] Understand your role's KPIs and how success is measured

### 60 Days

- [ ] Operating at 75-80% productivity level
- [ ] Own 2-3 significant projects or accounts
- [ ] Demonstrate proficiency with all required tools and systems
- [ ] Provide valuable input in team planning and strategy sessions
- [ ] Mentor or assist newer team members
- [ ] Identify and begin implementing at least one process improvement

### 90 Days

- [ ] Fully ramped and operating at 100% productivity
- [ ] Consistently meeting role expectations and KPIs
- [ ] Taking initiative on new projects without prompting
- [ ] Contributing to team culture and knowledge sharing
- [ ] Participating in hiring (interviews, referrals)
- [ ] Complete 90-day performance review with manager

## Department-Specific Onboarding

### Engineering Onboarding

**Additional Tools & Access:**

- GitHub organization membership with appropriate repository access
- AWS console access (role-based permissions)
- CI/CD pipelines: CircleCI for automated testing and deployment
- Monitoring: Datadog (infrastructure), Sentry (error tracking), PagerDuty (on-call)
- Development environment setup: Docker, local database, API keys
- Code review process and style guide documentation

**Week 1 Engineering Tasks:**

- Set up local development environment
- Deploy your first change to staging environment
- Review architecture documentation and system design docs
- Attend engineering all-hands and team standups
- Fix your first bug or implement a small feature

### Sales Onboarding

**Additional Tools & Access:**

- Salesforce CRM with full pipeline visibility
- Outreach.io for automated email sequences
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Gong for call recording and coaching
- Sales playbook and pitch deck library
- Demo environment with sample data

**Week 1 Sales Tasks:**

- Shadow 5 customer calls or demos
- Complete product demo certification
- Practice pitch with sales manager
- Learn qualification framework (MEDDPICC)
- Review top deal wins and losses

### Customer Success Onboarding

**Additional Tools & Access:**

- Zendesk for ticket management
- Intercom for in-app messaging
- ChurnZero for customer health monitoring
- Access to customer accounts (view-only initially)
- Playbooks for common customer scenarios

**Week 1 CS Tasks:**

- Shadow 3 customer onboarding calls
- Review top escalations and resolutions
- Learn product troubleshooting procedures
- Understand support SLAs and response protocols
- Practice responding to common support tickets

### Product Onboarding

**Additional Tools & Access:**

- Figma for design collaboration
- Productboard for roadmap planning
- Mixpanel and Amplitude for analytics
- UserTesting.com for research
- Access to customer feedback channels

**Week 1 Product Tasks:**

- Review current product roadmap
- Analyze top feature requests and usage data
- Shadow 3 customer research sessions
- Study competitor products hands-on
- Propose one small product improvement

### Marketing Onboarding

**Additional Tools & Access:**

- HubSpot for marketing automation
- Google Analytics and SEMrush
- Content calendar and brand guidelines
- Canva for design assets
- Social media management tools

**Week 1 Marketing Tasks:**

- Review content library and brand voice
- Analyze top-performing campaigns
- Audit competitor marketing presence
- Draft your first blog post or email
- Attend customer interview for case study

## Onboarding Checklist

### Administrative

- [ ] Complete all HR paperwork (I-9, W-4, benefits enrollment)
- [ ] Sign employee handbook and code of conduct
- [ ] Set up direct deposit for payroll
- [ ] Upload profile photo and bio to employee directory
- [ ] Confirm emergency contacts are accurate

### Technology

- [ ] Receive and set up company laptop and equipment
- [ ] Configure email, calendar, and Slack
- [ ] Install required software and applications
- [ ] Set up 1Password and store credentials securely
- [ ] Complete VPN setup for secure remote access
- [ ] Test access to all required systems and tools

### Training & Compliance

- [ ] Complete security awareness training (all 3 parts)
- [ ] Finish GDPR and data privacy training
- [ ] Watch product demo videos
- [ ] Complete NimbusHR product certification
- [ ] Review employee handbook thoroughly
- [ ] Acknowledge receipt of company policies

### Relationships

- [ ] Meet with onboarding buddy (multiple times)
- [ ] 1:1 with direct manager
- [ ] Coffee chats with 5+ cross-functional colleagues
- [ ] Attend team introduction meeting
- [ ] Meet your department leadership
- [ ] Join company social channels and groups

### Learning

- [ ] Review company mission, vision, and values
- [ ] Understand organizational structure
- [ ] Learn about key customers and use cases
- [ ] Study competitive landscape
- [ ] Read recent company announcements and blog posts
- [ ] Explore internal knowledge base and documentation

## Tips for Success

1. **Ask Questions**: There are no silly questions. Everyone wants to help you succeed.
2. **Take Notes**: You'll receive a lot of information—document what you learn.
3. **Be Patient**: Feeling overwhelmed is normal. Give yourself grace during the learning curve.
4. **Build Relationships**: Schedule informal chats with colleagues across departments.
5. **Seek Feedback Early**: Don't wait for formal reviews—ask for input regularly.
6. **Contribute Ideas**: Fresh perspectives are valuable—share your observations.
7. **Maintain Work-Life Balance**: Set boundaries and prioritize your wellbeing from day one.
8. **Stay Curious**: Keep learning about our industry, customers, and product.
